Healthcare Coverage State by State in the US

This study shows what percent of each state has healthcare coverage and what the makeup of each insurance type is in each state

Source: KFF https://www.kff.org/2fdbf6d/

To create my data pipeline, I followed these steps:

  1. Load CSV Data: Open and read the CSV file located at data/refined_data.csv.
  2. Extract Headers: Retrieve the first row from the CSV to use as keys for creating dictionaries.
  3. Transform Data: For each subsequent row, map the data to the headers, forming a dictionary for each row.
  4. Save to JSON: Compile all the dictionaries into a list and write this list to a new file, data/refined_data.json, in a structured JSON format.
  5. Close Files: Ensure all file operations are properly closed after reading and writing data.

Important Vocabulary:

  • Healthcare: The organized provision of medical care to individuals or a community through services offered by medical professionals.
  • Medicaid: A government insurance program for persons of all ages whose income and resources are insufficient to pay for health care.
  • Medicare: A federal system of health insurance for people over 65 years of age and for certain younger people with disabilities.
  • Employer: Refers to health insurance coverage provided by an individual's employer, typically as part of employee benefits.
  • Military: Health care coverage provided by military services, which includes the Department of Defense's TRICARE and care from Veterans Health Administration.

Prototypes:

In my project, I focused on turning complex data into clear, simple visuals that anyone can understand quickly. I used a geo chart because it shows state-by-state data in a way that's familiar to Americans. I also chose a pie chart to display percentages because it matches well with how people visualize proportions. However, I'm thinking about changing from a circular to a rectangular pie chart to make the differences between states stand out more, even with few data points. In terms of feedback, I was told to add graph titles, provide averages for people to compare against in the micro pages, and create a standard color palette. In addition, Mr. Gohde suggested that in my micro page, I switch from a circular pie chart to a rectangular/bar pie chart.
